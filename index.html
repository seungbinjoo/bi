Language-conditioned and Generalisable Bimanual Robotic Manipulation with Reinforcement Learning

Seung-Bin Joo1,2, João F. Henriques1,2
1University of Oxford, 2Visual Geometry Group
Paper Code

Abstract

Generalisable robotic manipulation is a key challenge in robot learning. For robots to be successfully deployed to diverse real-life manipulation scenarios, they must adapt to variations in object mass, shape, friction coefficient, and external disturbance forces on the robot. We present Rapid Motor Adaptation for Robot Manipulator Arms (RMA2) to solve this problem. RMA2 trains a reinforcement learning (RL) policy with high amounts of domain randomisation, and subsequently learns an adaptation module that infers privileged information about the environment during deployment using the robot’s past dynamic behaviour and depth images. This allows the robot to specifically adapt to different situations despite lacking direct knowledge of environment parameters such as object mass and friction.

While RMA2 demonstrates generalisable dextrous manipulation, it lacks in terms of imbuing robots with a semantic understanding of the objects being manipulated. Thus, we aim to extend our RL work on robotic manipulation by integrating the notion of semantics into the underlying manipulation tasks. We take inspiration from RL-VLM-F and incorporate vision-language model feedback. By integrating semantic priors into RMA2, we aim to explore language-conditioned RL policies which exhibit the ability to generalise to variations in both environment parameters and semantic attributes such as object colour, shape, and category.

RMA2

RMA2 involves 3 stages. First, we train a robust base policy conditioned on privileged environment parameters. Secondly, the adaptation module predicts the privileged environment parameters using proprioceptive history and depth information. The adaptation module is learned by regressing to the ground truth environment information produced by the base policy. Lastly, during evaluation, we take the trained base policy and adapter for real-life deployment.
